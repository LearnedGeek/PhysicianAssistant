## 1. Intellectual Property — The Most Important Point

I want to start by saying clearly: **this idea is yours.** You conceived it. You've been working on it since before the pandemic. You tried to make it happen with local operators and never gave up. That vision — an AI physician's assistant that helps patients when the doctor isn't available — that is your contribution, and I respect it deeply. Without your idea and your vision, we wouldn't be having this conversation.

But I need to be transparent about something important, and I say this as a partner who wants to protect what we're building together: under both US law and Peruvian law, intellectual property protection attaches to the **implementation** — the code, the architecture, the research, the documentation — not to the concept or idea.

This isn't my preference or my opinion. I consulted with Peruvian lawyers specifically on this point. Peru's Decreto Legislativo 822 and the recent Ley 32314 (2025) are explicit: the author is the person who creates the intellectual work. The US Copyright Office and USPTO follow the same principle. An idea cannot be registered as IP in either country. The implementation can.

**But here's why this matters practically — not just legally:**

Someone has to maintain this platform every single day. Consider what happens over the life of this project:

| Ongoing Responsibility | Who Does It | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Server hosting and uptime | Mark / Learned Geek | 24/7/365 |
| Security patches and updates | Mark / Learned Geek | Weekly |
| Bug fixes (including 2am emergencies) | Mark / Learned Geek | Ongoing |
| AI API costs (Anthropic, PubMed) | Mark / Learned Geek | Monthly |
| Messaging costs (Twilio/WhatsApp) | Mark / Learned Geek | Monthly |
| Professional liability insurance | Mark / Learned Geek | Annual |
| Cyber liability insurance | Mark / Learned Geek | Annual |
| Feature development and improvements | Mark / Learned Geek | Ongoing |
| Database management and backups | Mark / Learned Geek | Daily |
| Compliance updates (Ley 29733, ARCO) | Mark / Learned Geek | As required |

If the IP is in a shared entity, who is accountable for all of this? The answer is still me — through Learned Geek. The entity that maintains, secures, insures, and operates the platform needs to own what it maintains. Otherwise, I am effectively an employee working on someone else's platform — not a partner.

**What I'm proposing protects both of us.** If Learned Geek owns and maintains the platform, DrOk always has a working, secure, insured product. You never have to worry about the technology — that's my responsibility and my commitment. You focus on what you do brilliantly: the clinical vision, the physician network, the regulatory navigation, and growing DrOk.

**Your concern about opportunities:** Any opportunity that comes through DrOk — through your physicians, your network, your market — we share proportionally per our agreement. The IP structure doesn't change that. It simply ensures the engine that powers everything stays maintained and operational.
