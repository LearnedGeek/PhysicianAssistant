## Proposal Summary

**1. Intellectual Property**
I retain ownership of the platform IP under my company (Learned Geek LLC) and grant you an exclusive 24-month license to operate DrOk. This protects both of us — you have exclusivity, I maintain the ability to sustain and evolve the platform.

**2. DrOk Partnership**
We form a partnership for DrOk where you and I are the principal partners. I contribute the platform, research, and infrastructure. You contribute your medical expertise, physician network, and commercial development.

**3. About Carlos**
I deeply value Carlos's contribution. My proposal is that Carlos participates as an **advisor** with fair compensation for his legal work — either a fixed fee or a small equity stake. But the operational partners in this business are you and me. This simplifies decision-making and is standard practice in technology startups.

**4. Initial Investment**
I need an investment from you to cover development costs. I propose phasing it to keep it manageable:

| Phase | Investment |
|---|---|
| Discovery + kickoff | $2,500 |
| Phase 2 — Product chatbot | $2,500 |
| Phase 3 — Triage system | $2,500 |
| Phase 4 — Dashboard + launch | $2,500 |
| **Total** | **$10,000** |

This is not a payment for IP — it's your investment in the project that demonstrates mutual commitment. I am investing significantly more in time, infrastructure, and operating costs.

**5. Revenue — We Both Win**
Your income grows with the success of the business:

- **Per-physician monthly fee** — split between us per our equity share
- **As you bring more physicians, you earn more** — your commercial effort is directly reflected in your income
- **Platform license fee** — a portion of each subscription covers infrastructure and maintenance costs (goes to Learned Geek)
- **Long-term, we both build real wealth** if this scales the way we believe it can

**6. Proposed Equity Split**
My initial proposal is **55% Mark / 45% Martin** in the DrOk partnership. The difference reflects my prior investment (6+ months of research, the working POC, all architecture and documentation), but as the business grows, your commercial contributions balance the equation. Full details are in the attached document.
