## Minimum Subscription Pricing

Martin sets the physician subscription price — he knows his market. However, there is a **minimum floor** to ensure operating costs are covered regardless of price:

| Component | Amount | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Platform license fee** | $15–20/physician/month | Covers infrastructure (Azure, Anthropic API, Twilio, insurance, maintenance) — goes to Learned Geek |
| **Minimum subscription price** | $50/physician/month | Below this, operating costs consume too much of the revenue margin |
| **Recommended range** | $75–150/physician/month | Based on comparable medical SaaS platforms in Latin America |

**How it works:** If Martin charges $100/physician/month, the first $15–20 covers platform costs (Learned Geek), and the remaining $80–85 is net revenue split 55/45. Martin cannot set a price below $50/month without mutual agreement — this ensures neither partner subsidizes the other's operating costs.

Martin is free to charge **above** the recommended range if his market supports it. A Harvard-affiliated pediatrician selling an AI triage platform backed by PubMed evidence may command a premium. The higher the price, the more both partners earn.
