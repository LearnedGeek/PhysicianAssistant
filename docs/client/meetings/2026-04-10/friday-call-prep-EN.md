## The Key Structure to Confirm

### The SRL (Peruvian Entity)

Based on Ani's research and the summary sent to Erika/Jocho, the proposed structure is:

**Martin creates a Peruvian SRL (Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada)** — this is like a US LLC but under Peruvian law. This entity:
- Holds the DrOk brand and IP registration (this is what Martin wanted)
- Is the commercial entity that contracts with physicians in Peru
- Is co-owned 50/50 by Mark and Martin
- Handles Peru-side taxes, compliance, and regulatory obligations

**Why SRL and not SAC?**
- SRL is simpler, cheaper to form, fewer regulatory requirements
- Good for 2 partners, no board of directors needed
- Adequate for the scale we're starting at
- Can convert to SAC later if needed for investors or growth

### Mark's LLC (Learned Geek)

- Invoices the SRL for development, maintenance, and hosting services
- Receives the $10,000 phased investment as consulting/maintenance fees
- Maintains and operates the platform (servers, security, updates, insurance)
- Has a **perpetual, royalty-free license-back** for all code Mark writes — this is critical

### The License-Back (Your Key Protection)

This is the structural protection that replaces "IP stays with Learned Geek":

- The SRL owns the IP registration (Martin is happy)
- Mark's LLC has a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use, modify, and maintain all code he wrote
- This means: even if the partnership dissolves, Mark can still maintain the platform, fix bugs, and support existing users
- **Maintainer veto:** Any code changes, new features, or outsourcing development needs Mark's written approval (first right of refusal at same price)

**Why this works for both:**
- Martin: "I co-own the IP" — yes, through the SRL
- Mark: "I can never be locked out of my own code" — yes, through the license-back
- Same practical outcome as before, different framing
