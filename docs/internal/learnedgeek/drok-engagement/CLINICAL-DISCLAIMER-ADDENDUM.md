This Clinical Disclaimer Addendum ("Addendum") supplements the Master Services Agreement ("MSA") between the Parties. In the event of conflict, this Addendum governs with respect to clinical matters.

---

## 1. Nature of the Platform

1.1 The DrOk platform is a **technology tool** that assists physicians in organizing, prioritizing, and responding to patient communications. It is not a medical device, diagnostic system, or clinical decision-making system.

1.2 The platform generates **preliminary clinical impressions** ("impresión diagnóstica") based on patient-reported symptoms and published medical literature. These impressions are informational inputs to the physician's clinical judgment — not diagnoses, prescriptions, or treatment recommendations.

1.3 No output of the platform — including triage classifications, urgency assessments, clinical impressions, or literature references — constitutes medical advice or replaces the independent clinical judgment of a licensed physician.

---

## 2. AI Limitations

2.1 The platform uses artificial intelligence (AI) language models to process patient communications and generate clinical impressions. **AI systems may produce incorrect, incomplete, or misleading outputs** (referred to as "confabulation" or "hallucination").

2.2 Provider has implemented architectural safeguards to mitigate confabulation risk (the "Authenticity Boundary"), including confidence thresholding, citation enforcement, null response paths, and deterministic emergency detection. These safeguards reduce — but do not eliminate — the risk of incorrect AI output.

2.3 **Client acknowledges and accepts that no AI system can guarantee 100% accuracy.** The physician review and approval process ("VoBo") exists precisely because AI outputs require human clinical validation.

---

## 3. Clinical Responsibility

3.1 **Client is solely responsible for all clinical decisions** made using, informed by, or in response to any output of the platform.

3.2 Client shall review and approve (VoBo) all AI-generated clinical impressions before they inform any patient communication or clinical action.

3.3 Client shall not rely on the platform as the sole basis for any clinical decision, including but not limited to: diagnosis, treatment, referral, prescription, or emergency escalation.

3.4 Client is responsible for establishing and maintaining the emergency detection keyword list and critical lab value thresholds used by the platform. Provider implements these thresholds as configured by Client but makes no clinical judgment about their adequacy.

---

## 4. Emergency Limitations

4.1 The platform includes an emergency detection pathway that uses deterministic keyword matching and threshold-based rules to identify potentially urgent situations. **This pathway is not a substitute for clinical triage by a licensed physician.**

4.2 The emergency detection pathway may produce false positives (flagging non-emergencies) or false negatives (failing to flag true emergencies). Client acknowledges this limitation.

4.3 The platform's default behavior is to escalate uncertain cases rather than underestimate urgency. Client is responsible for configuring appropriate escalation thresholds consistent with their standard of care.

---

## 5. Indemnification

5.1 Client shall indemnify and hold Provider harmless from any and all claims, damages, losses, or liabilities arising from:
- Clinical decisions made by Client or Client's staff using or informed by platform outputs
- Patient outcomes resulting from clinical decisions
- Client's failure to review AI-generated outputs before acting on them
- Regulatory actions taken against Client related to clinical practice

5.2 This indemnification is in addition to, and does not limit, the indemnification provisions of the MSA.

## 6. Regulatory Classification

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that the classification of the platform under Peruvian regulatory frameworks (including DIGEMID medical device classification and Ley 30421 telemedicine law) is pending as of the date of this Addendum.

6.2 If the platform is classified as a medical device or falls under telemedicine regulation, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to amend this Addendum and the MSA to reflect any additional obligations. Client is responsible for regulatory compliance costs in their jurisdiction per MSA Section 9.
