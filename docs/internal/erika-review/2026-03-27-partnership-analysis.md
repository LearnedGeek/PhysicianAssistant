## What Happened

After a productive meeting with Martin and his lawyer Carlos Rojas (Rebaza, Alcázar & De Las Casas — top-tier Peruvian firm), Martin sent an unsolicited WhatsApp message proposing an equity partnership — all three as co-owners. He came to Mark first before telling Carlos.

> "No lo hemos conversado formalmente, pero si me gustaría en algún momento hablar de la posibilidad de ser socios en este emprendimiento... Los porcentajes y demás los podemos verlos después, pero la definición es clara, podemos ser socios?"

Translation: "Can we be partners? We'll figure out percentages later." He also said if Peru is complicated, they can start in the US market. No pressure, no commitment.

---

## Mark's Position

Open to partnership conceptually. Concerns:

1. **IP protection** — platform is Mark's primary asset; diluting ownership kills buyout potential
2. **Cash flow** — can't work for months with no income; has real costs (~$7K/yr infrastructure)
3. **Sunk costs** — ~$52,500 in time already invested (ANI research, POC, architecture, docs)
4. **Risk asymmetry** — if it fails, Mark loses time AND money; Martin loses only time
5. **Martin may not have cash** — he proposed equity (not payment) and may be bringing Carlos in partly for funding. Karen (Mark's wife) flagged this concern.

Mark wants to be equitable — he's not trying to extract maximum value. He wants a structure where everyone is invested and protected.

## Proposed Structure: Two-Entity Model

```
Learned Geek LLC (100% Mark)
    │
    │  licenses platform to ──►  DrOk JV Entity (Mark 55% + Martin 45%)
    │                                │
    │  ◄── license fees ────────────┘
    │  ◄── build fee ───────────────┘
    │
    └── Retains: all platform IP, codebase, architecture
        Can license to other brands/markets independently
```

**Learned Geek LLC** (100% Mark, forever):
- Owns ALL platform IP
- Licenses to DrOk under commercial license
- Gets paid: build fee + $15/physician/month platform license
- Can white-label to other markets independently

**DrOk JV Entity** (55% Mark, 45% Martin):
- Owns the DrOk brand and physician relationships
- Operates the business: sales, onboarding, clinical validation
- Pays Learned Geek for development and licensing
- Revenue from subscriptions splits per equity

**Carlos:** Advisor with 3-5% equity grant (vests 24 months). Not a co-founder or governance partner. His contribution is advisory, not operational.

## Non-Negotiables

1. Learned Geek retains 100% platform IP — license only, never assignment
2. Martin invests cash (phased or upfront) — not free
3. All equity vests over 24 months with 6-month cliff
4. Sales performance gate — 10 paying physicians in 12 months or exclusivity lapses
5. Mark has technical authority on platform decisions
6. Platform buyout is separate from DrOk acquisition
7. Carlos is advisor (3-5%), not co-founder
